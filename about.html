About Us

A nonpartisan political organization encouraging informed and active participation in government across Newport County.

Our Mission

The League of Women Voters of Newport County is a nonpartisan political organization that encourages informed and active participation in government, works to increase understanding of major public policy issues, and influences public policy through education and advocacy.

Our History

The League of Women Voters has championed voting rights and civic engagement for over 100 years, growing from the suffrage movement into a powerful force for democracy.

Key Milestones

  • 1917: Rhode Island passes "presidential suffrage" law
  • January 6, 1920: Rhode Island ratifies 19th Amendment (ahead of national deadline)
  • October 8, 1920: League of Women Voters of Rhode Island officially organized
  • 1920-1921: League grows to 1,000+ members in first year
  • 1920s onward: Newport County chapter established as part of statewide expansion

Today, LWV Newport County continues this legacy through voter registration, candidate forums, and advocacy for transparent, accessible government across six Rhode Island communities.

Our Role in the League Structure

When joining a local LWV, one also becomes a member of the State and National LWV.

LWVUS (National)

  • Develops and adopts national public policy positions
  • Advocates at the federal level (Congress, federal agencies)
  • Provides branding, training, legal guidance, and organizational standards
  • Charters state and local leagues
  • Coordinates national initiatives (e.g., voting rights campaigns)
  • National positions are binding on all state and local leagues

LWVRI (State League)

  • Operates at the state level and coordinates local leagues within Rhode Island
  • Advocates on state legislation and policy issues
  • Develops state-level positions through member study and consensus
  • Supports and trains local leagues
  • Ensures local leagues comply with LWV policies and bylaws
  • May take action on issues not covered at national level
  • Must align with national positions but can adopt additional positions specific to Rhode Island

LWVNC (Newport County - Local League)

  • Grassroots implementation and community engagement
  • Conducts voter registration drives and candidate forums
  • Advocates on local issues (school boards, city councils, counties)
  • Develops local positions through member study and consensus
  • Educates voters in our community
  • Must support national and state positions, cannot contradict them
  • May act only within adopted positions

What We Do

Voter Services

We register voters, host candidate forums, and provide nonpartisan voter education resources to help citizens make informed decisions.

Advocacy

We advocate for policies that protect voting rights, improve government transparency, and strengthen democracy at all levels.

Community Engagement

We bring people together through educational programs, forums, and events that build civic participation and democratic values.

Statement of Nonpartisanship

The League of Women Voters Newport County is a nonpartisan political organization that encourages informed and active participation in government, works to increase understanding of major public policy issues and influences public policy through education and advocacy. We take action on public policy positions established through member agreement. We do not support or oppose any political party or candidate.

LWVNC recognizes that the League experience uniquely equips members for public life at many different levels and encourages members to use their knowledge and abilities on an individual basis. Non-board members are encouraged to participate fully in partisan activities.

Board Member Guidelines:

  • The President and Vice President shall not undertake any action that will cause them to be identified publicly as supporting or opposing any candidate for office or any political party.
  • Board members shall not run for or hold partisan elective office.
  • League notices announcing the resignation of a board member to run for public office should be carefully worded to avoid the appearance of endorsing the candidacy of the resigning board member.
  • A board member may not speak publicly against a League position.
  • A board member may serve on any public board, commission, committee or coalition. However, that board member does not represent the League unless officially designated as a League representative by the League board.
  • The political activities of a spouse or relative of a board member are considered separate and distinct from the activities of that board member.
